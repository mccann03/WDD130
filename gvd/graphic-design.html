Basic

Info

Graphic design is the arrangement and creation of elements in a manner that is visually pleasing to the viewer. That said the term graphic design, while having remained mostly unchanged has been altered in our modern day and age to mean something slightly different than what it has meant in decades past. Now a days graphic design means more than working with a pen and paper, with the rise of computers it has also become necessary for one to have a decent understanding of technology to become a graphic designer. Additionally, computers have also opened more types of jobs such as photo editor and multimedia designer, that are focused on certain aspects of graphic design and deal with their own specific technology used to make it.

Aspects

At its core graphic design is made of various aspects that can be broken up into eight different elements, which include line, shape, form, texture, space, imagery, typography, and color. Lines are considered the basis of graphic design as they can be used for anything, from outlining an object to dividing content. Shape can be considered an extension of line, as it involves using lines to make object of various appearances and designs. Form is the aspect of graphic design that involves giving depth to shapes, to make them three dimensional. Texture creates an even deeper level of depth on three-dimensional objects by creating surface quality or imperfections in the surface of the object, which brings a higher level of realism to it. Space is the usage of background and design, and the balance achieved in a graphic design from proper application of the two. Imagery is the use of images in a design and placing them in a manner that brings out the best in that design. Typography is similar to imagery; however, it differs in that it makes use of words and fonts rather images. Finally, color is the aspect that is responsible for bringing out the right amount of similarity and contrast in an image to make it great.
