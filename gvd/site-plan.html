Content

Home page

Graphic design is defined as the skill of creating and arranging visual elements in things like books, advertisements, and websites in a manner that either visually entices or stimulates the looker. This website focuses on improving one’s graphic designing abilities by teaching them skills dealing with graphic design through a variety of classes that might help to improve their skills or understanding of this concept. To be more a little bit more specific these classes will provide help and guidance with one’s artistry skills, explain about the technology used regarding graphic design, and demonstrate how to use the previous two skills to create things such as websites, online advertisement, and visuals in computer games.

Graphic Design

Basic info
Graphic design is the arrangement and creation of elements in a manner that is visually pleasing to the viewer. That said the term graphic design, while having remained mostly unchanged has been altered in our modern day and age to mean something slightly different than what it has meant in decades past. Now a days graphic design means more than working with a pen and paper, with the rise of computers it has also become necessary for one to have a decent understanding of technology to become a graphic designer. Additionally, computers have also opened more types of jobs such as photo editor and multimedia designer, that are focused on certain aspects of graphic design and deal with their own specific technology used to make it.

Aspects
At its core graphic design is made of various aspects that can be broken up into eight different elements, which include line, shape, form, texture, space, imagery, typography, and color. Lines are considered the basis of graphic design as they can be used for anything, from outlining an object to dividing content. Shape can be considered an extension of line, as it involves using lines to make object of various appearances and designs. Form is the aspect of graphic design that involves giving depth to shapes, to make them three dimensional. Texture creates an even deeper level of depth on three-dimensional objects by creating surface quality or imperfections in the surface of the object, which brings a higher level of realism to it. Space is the usage of background and design, and the balance achieved in a graphic design from proper application of the two. Imagery is the use of images in a design and placing them in a manner that brings out the best in that design. Typography is similar to imagery; however, it differs in that it makes use of words and fonts rather images. Finally, color is the aspect that is responsible for bringing out the right amount of similarity and contrast in an image to make it great.

Classes

Art
This class teaches the fundamentals of art including color, contrast, balance, value, and form, and how to apply these principles to graphic design. Additionally, classes will be taught with computers so that students can get a better understanding of how to use them for graphic design, and how software is used in the design process. Lastly, this class will provide students with an environment that will encourage their creative growth, and expression of individuality in their own unique way through independent study assignment when they may express their creativity in any way they desire.

Technology
The technology class teaches various types of hardware and software that are used to design things such as websites and online advertisements. Furthermore, instructors when teaching about these technologies will also provide hands on useful suggestions and tips in addition to hands on support when need, that should make their usage easier for those that have little or even no experience using computers. After this class students should have the ability to create webpages without any help using the most recent graphic design software and hardware.

Website Design
This class focuses on helping individuals build detailed and beautiful webpages and teaches concepts similar to the ones taught in the two classes above, but more in-depth and complex. Things that will be taught in this course include how to alter website programming, what different parts of the code do, and how and what one can do in this code to make a website more applying. Finally, at the end of this course student will be allowed to put what they want to the test by putting together a website of their own that will display everything they have learned, and challenge them to show to themselves and everyone else that they can go beyond expectations.
